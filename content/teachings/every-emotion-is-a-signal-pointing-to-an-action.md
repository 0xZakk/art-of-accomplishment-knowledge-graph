Joe opens this lesson by naming the core problem: most of us were never taught what emotions are, and were actively or passively taught to avoid them. His reframe is that each emotion is a signal carrying useful information, and the more we're willing to be with our emotions, the better our lives become.

> "Most of us were never taught what emotions were. We were actually actively taught or passively taught to avoid them... The more we're with them, the better our life is."

Across all eleven emotions he describes, the same logic holds: the feeling is pointing somewhere. Anger points to a boundary you want to draw and what you care about. Anxiety points to something you know needs doing. Jealousy is "a beautiful road map" to what you really want. Disgust flags something toxic you've taken in. Contempt reveals a blind spot in your own shadow. Guilt signals that you've stopped listening to your wants. Even fear is either excitement asking for breath or a signal that something needs care.

The practical upshot is to stop treating emotions as malfunctions to suppress and start reading them as instructions. The discomfort is information about an action to take, a need to meet, a boundary to set, or a truth to acknowledge. Suppressing the feeling means losing the message.
