The endpoint of letting emotions move is fluidity. Joe describes a practice he calls emotional inquiry—using wonder, investigation, and experimentation to learn how each emotion feels in the body—until, after maybe a year of staying open, the emotions stop being something that grabs you. With no resistance, they no longer control you. Instead, they inform you.

Each emotion, felt cleanly, carries a signal. Anger points to a boundary or something that isn't working. Fear points to an unmet need or something you're not admitting to yourself.

> "Once they become fluid, they give you the signal and then you get to decide, hey, I don't have to react to it. I'm not taken by it. I don't need to manage it. I can just be with it."

This is the difference between an emotion as a command and an emotion as a message. When you can hang out with the feeling rather than obey it or fight it, you get to read its wisdom and then choose whether the signal is worth acting on right now. The energy that was spent clamping down—"this aspect of me isn't okay"—becomes available for actually living the life you want.
