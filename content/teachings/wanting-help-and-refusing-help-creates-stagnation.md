The man has been stuck in the same financial, love, and life position for a decade, and he experiences it as needing more help while also being driven to help everyone around him. Joe finds the engine of the stuckness in that contradiction: one part wants to be helped, another part wants to do the helping, and they pull in opposite directions.

> "One part of you is like I want to be helped and it's going in one direction and one part of you is like I want to help and it's going in a different direction and it's creating stagnation in your life."

The two impulses cancel. He hires marketers (wanting help) but then resists and over-functions, giving them everything while quietly not letting them actually help him. The result is a system that strains in both directions and goes nowhere.

Joe's diagnostic is brutal and clarifying: look at whether the help you sought actually arrived. When every helper fails to deliver, that's not bad luck—it's evidence the underlying want for help isn't whole.

> "That's part of how you know somebody doesn't want help is all the help that they try to get for themselves fail."
