Joe argues that the formula for business success and the formula for spiritual development are identical: embrace intensity, deepen connection, and discern internal alignment. Every obstacle that slows down a business — second-guessing, conflict avoidance, defensiveness — also slows down personal growth. They share the same root cause: emotional avoidance.

The practical implication is that finding misalignment in your business and correcting it is "like sweeping thousand dollar bills off the floor." When a company has excessive second-guessing, it means there's an emotional experience being avoided. When there's conflict avoidance, it means fear is running the show. The work of putting these into alignment is simultaneously business strategy and spiritual practice.

> "If you really get the concept that business can be a self-discovery journey and that the more ways you find for your business to be in alignment with who you are, the more successful you can be."

This isn't about adding spiritual practices on top of business — it's recognizing they were never separate to begin with.
