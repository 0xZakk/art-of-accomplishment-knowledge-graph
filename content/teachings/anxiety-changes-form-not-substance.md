A group of wealthy founders feared that losing their resources would force them back into anxiety-driven patterns. Joe's observation: they're already in anxiety — it just changed form. The anxiety of "I need to get money" became "I'm afraid to lose money." The anxiety of "I'm incomplete without X" became "What if X goes away?"

This pattern applies to everything people attach their freedom to. The object changes but the underlying anxiety remains constant. Money, relationships, health, spiritual attainment — each becomes something to fear losing the moment you believe it's required for your well-being.

> "If they're scared that they need something to be happy, the anxiety hasn't changed — it's just changed forms."
