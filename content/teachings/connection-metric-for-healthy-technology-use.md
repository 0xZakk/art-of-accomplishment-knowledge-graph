For using AI in personal development, Joe offers a single felt metric and one firm rule. The rule: anything you're willing to talk to an AI about, be willing to talk to a human about too — a friend, a 12-step group, a church confession, anyone. People who pair AI work with real human conversation transform far faster than those talking only to a machine.

The metric is the same one he uses to distinguish a cult or an abusive relationship from healthy growth: which direction is your connection trending? Are you becoming more isolated, less safe in the world, more conditionally safe with the one object of safety — or are you connecting more authentically with the people in your life, having hard conversations openheartedly, and seeing more opportunity rather than less? The same applies to your relationship with yourself.

> "If you're interacting with AI in a way that leaves you feeling like social media — no bueno."

Joe contrasts the post-scrolling pit in your stomach with how you feel after a great hike with friends or even a hard meditation retreat. The instruction is to keep iterating on how you use the technology until it reliably leaves you feeling more expansive, more authentic, and more whole — distinct from an AI that's merely trying to make you feel good in the moment, which eventually won't.

> "Keep on iterating with the way that you're interacting with AI until you start finding ways that make you feel better and better and better."
