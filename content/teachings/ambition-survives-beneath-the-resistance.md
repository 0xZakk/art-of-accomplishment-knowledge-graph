Underneath the sadness, the resistance, and the self-rejection, the drive to go for what you want is still intact. It hasn't been destroyed by years of being pushed down—it's been buried, not killed. Once the man welcomes himself, Joe directs him to feel that ambition, and it's immediately present.

> "Even though your ambition is been pushed down, down behind a whole bunch of [stuff], I see it and I really admire it. I appreciate it. I look forward to seeing it just set free."

Crucially, Joe reframes the very act of showing up as evidence that the ambition is already in motion. The person didn't have to manufacture drive; he was already enacting it.

> "And you on this call right now is an action of ambition. You going for it on this call right now as an act and an action of ambition."

Ambition here isn't a craving or a should. It's the natural forward pull of a person—admirable, alive, and ready to move the moment it stops being rejected.
