Joe argues that giving things away for free — whether coaching, food, or clothing — can teach people they can't take care of themselves. Real transformation requires an exchange that is *felt*. For a billionaire, $200 isn't a felt exchange. For someone earning $10/hour, $200 is enormous. The exchange must be proportional to create genuine investment.

He references a quote from an indigenous tribe in "The Soul of Money": "If you're here to help me, no thank you. If you're here to work together for our mutual freedom, let's get to work." Free content doesn't generate the same completion rates, buy-in, or transformation as content that requires something from the participant.

> "If you're here to help me, no thank you. If you're here to work together for our mutual freedom, let's get to work."
