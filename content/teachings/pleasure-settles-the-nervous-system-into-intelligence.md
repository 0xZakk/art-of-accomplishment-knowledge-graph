If fight-or-flight is what collapses your intelligence, pleasure is what restores it — because the two states are mutually exclusive. You simply can't be in fight-or-flight while taking pleasure in something.

> "You can't be in fight or flight when you're in pleasure."

Joe is careful to say he doesn't mean elaborate, expensive pleasure — a spa, a massage, yoga and a facial all at once. He means the small, immediate kind available right now: a little more pleasure in watching the video, a little more pleasure in your next breath. That tiny shift is enough to feel the nervous system settle.

> "Right now, can you take a little more pleasure in the next breath that you have? And that immediately, you can feel it settles your nervous system, which makes you more intelligent."

So learning pleasure isn't indulgence; it's the practical lever for nervous-system intelligence. The more you can draw pleasure from simple things, the more access you have to the binary-free, catastrophe-free thinking that fear shuts down.
