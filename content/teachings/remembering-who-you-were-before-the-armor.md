During a treatment weekend, Will Chesney had a profound shift: "I just woke up and it was me again." He felt like the 18-year-old who was going to crush SEAL training — the fire, the unstoppability, the person before ego, trauma, and accumulated grief had buried him. "Nothing was going to stop me. The Devil himself could have walked into that room and I'd have been like, we're good."

Brett notes this is a common pattern across many contexts: a moment of re-remembering where you reconnect with who you were before the defenses were built. But the journey matters — the integration of the old self with everything that's happened creates something different from simply going back. Will woke up as himself, but without the need to drink, without feeling sorry for himself, with spiritual beliefs restored and a new relationship to his friends' deaths.

> "I woke up and it was me. Like legit me. Like who I was before I went through buds, when I felt like the 18-year-old me again."

This echoes the broader teaching that transformation is not building something new but uncovering what's already there — archaeology rather than invention.
