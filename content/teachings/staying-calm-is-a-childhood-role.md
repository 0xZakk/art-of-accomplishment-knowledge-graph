When Joe traces the man's compulsive calm back to its origin, it isn't a personality trait—it's a job he was assigned as a kid. His sister was the free, expressive, rebellious one who got in trouble constantly and created turmoil. The man's role became the opposite: stay out of the line of fire, and help everyone settle down afterward.

> "My role was to just not do that. And um not only not do that, but help things settle after."

Calm, in this light, was a survival strategy. By muting his own love, power, joy, and aggression, he kept himself safe and kept the peace around him. Joe names the full price: "Through not loving, not having your power and not being aggressive and not being joyful." The role worked in childhood, but as an adult it quietly costs him everything he most wants to express.

Seeing calm as an installed role—rather than who he is—is what frees the man to stop defending it.
