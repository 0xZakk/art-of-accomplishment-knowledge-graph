The woman in this session was a successful comedian — rewarded lavishly for performing. When she shifted to coaching (her authentic calling), the money dried up. Joe identifies the deeper pattern: her coaching is actually a continuation of the performance — she's still trying to "save" people (her parents) rather than genuinely connecting.

The career shift from comedy to coaching looked like a move toward authenticity, but the underlying relational pattern remained unchanged. She's coaching from the same place she did comedy — performing for connection rather than being herself. That's what blocks the money.

> "How much the coaching is actually just you still trying to save your parents? ... That's what's getting in the way of you making money coaching."

The insight is that authentic earning requires authentic being. When the service itself is still a performance — even a helping one — clients sense it and the exchange remains incomplete. The money block lifts when the person behind the work stops performing and starts being.
