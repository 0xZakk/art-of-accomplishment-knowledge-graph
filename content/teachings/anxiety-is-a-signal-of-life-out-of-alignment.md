Anxiety is not a malfunction to be silenced; it's a pointer. It marks the gap between the life you're living and the life that would actually fit you. Joe argues that getting rid of it isn't a breathing trick or a quick reset—it's the ongoing work of bringing your life back into alignment.

> "The anxiety is pointing to something. It's pointing to the way in which your life is out of alignment. And if you're not willing to do the work to get your life back into alignment, that anxiety is constantly going to be with you to remind you that your life is not in alignment."

The system reads chronic self-neglect as danger: if you're not taking care of yourself, some part of you concludes you must not be safe. Most of this anxiety, Joe says, comes from not doing the thing you know you want to be doing—and from not being who you are. The overwhelm was never about the volume of tasks.

> "The overwhelm never was by all the stuff you were doing. The anxiety wasn't from all the stuff that you had to do... It came from the fact that you weren't doing the things that you really wanted to be doing."

Treated this way, anxiety becomes information rather than an enemy: a reliable signal that something needs realigning.
