The single word in the title of this guide is "ouch." Joe's simplest experiment: close your eyes, listen to the voice in your head, speak whatever it says out loud, and say "ouch" after every critical thing it utters. It does two things at once.

First, it's neurological. A thought kept silent only activates the prefrontal cortex; spoken aloud, it lights up other parts of the brain, so you suddenly hear the criticism in a completely different light.

> "Holy crap, that's abusive as shit... I would never talk to a friend like this ever."

Second, the "ouch" makes you register the cost. You get to own how abusive the self-talk is — talk you'd never use on a friend, a kid, or a boss, yet somehow find acceptable to aim at yourself. Hearing it externalized breaks the spell of agreement and lets self-compassion in. It's a small mechanical practice with an outsized effect, because it converts an invisible internal stream into something you can actually evaluate.
