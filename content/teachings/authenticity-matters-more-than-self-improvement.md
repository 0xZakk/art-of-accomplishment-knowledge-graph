The fifth pattern is prioritizing self-improvement over authenticity. In his twenties, Joe chased enlightenment—a project he later saw was just his perfectionism in disguise, "porn for perfectionism," a fantasy of never being human or suffering again. Every teacher told him the same thing—you're already there, the thing chasing is the thing you seek—and he couldn't hear it.

What finally changed everything wasn't a perfect routine or body. It was understanding the truth of his own nature.

> "It happened because I just understood the truth about my nature. That was it. That's all I needed to do. And it was at that moment that I realized that, oh, everything was easier because I understood myself, because I was being myself."

Once he made his job simply to understand himself more deeply, most of what he'd been striving for arrived more easily. Knowing himself revealed that what he craved was connection—and having connection made his whole life easier. He became the person he used to want to be, without caring anymore that he'd wanted it.

The point isn't to fix yourself into someone better; it's to be yourself, which is what actually produces the change self-improvement keeps promising.
