The self-reliant person's struggle to ask for help has a hidden flip side: a detailed fantasy of exactly how they want to be helped that no one can possibly fulfill. Joe explains that you want to be helped the way a child wanted to be helped — perfectly attuned, mind-read, met at the exact right moment.

> "On one level you have a hard time asking for help but on another level you have this deep fantasy of exactly how you want to be helped and nobody can fulfill it."

Because that attunement was never delivered in childhood, the fantasy persists unchanged into adulthood. And its very persistence makes it impossible for anyone to satisfy, which keeps reinforcing the belief that you're alone and can't get the help you've always needed.

The same dynamic shows up in asking: people sabotage requests by only accepting help that's done "exactly right." Joe's prescription is to ask for help every day in small, simple ways — and to watch for the ways you set the ask up to fail.

> "The persistence of that fantasy makes it that it's incredibly hard for anybody to deliver it, which means you're always alone."
