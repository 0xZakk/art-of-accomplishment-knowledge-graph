This is the moment Joe says he looks for in a top performer: the recognition that nothing external is ever going to make them whole. Most people spend their lives trying to fill an empty space with achievement, money, fame, or love. The breakthrough is not finding the right thing to fill it with — it's stopping the chase entirely and turning toward the emptiness itself.

> "Oh, I'm not going to be made whole by anything externally. Maybe I'm never going to be made whole by anything."

Once that lands, the questions change completely. Instead of "what should I achieve next," it becomes: what is actually in that empty space I've been trying to fill? What's really going on there? How do I stop avoiding it and fall in love with it? Joe calls this the place where the transformational journey gets really good and where the whole identity can shift, because the very thing you organized your life to escape turns out to be the doorway.

> "The thing that I've been avoiding my whole life has been the most direct path to freedom that I've ever found."
