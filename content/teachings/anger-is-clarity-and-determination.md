Joe strips anger down to its essence: clarity and determination. The destructive or confusing forms anger usually takes are what happens when that clean energy gets distorted on the way out. He uses the image of a tube — when the tube is kinked or blocked, the same energy comes out sideways as a passive-aggressive jab ("Nice dress"), an attack ("You son of a..."), or denial ("I'm not angry").

> "Anger at its core is clarity and determination... But unblocked, it's just clarity and determination."

Unblocked, anger isn't a problem to manage; it's information about what matters to you. It points toward an action.

> "Anger points you to an action. What's a boundary you want to draw? What's something you want to say no to? What isn't working for you? What do you care about? It's telling you what you want to protect."

So when anger arises, rather than venting it sideways or suppressing it into "I'm not angry," the practice is to let it flow cleanly and read its message: the boundary it wants drawn, the no it wants spoken, the thing it wants to protect. Anger is the emotion that clarifies what you care about.
