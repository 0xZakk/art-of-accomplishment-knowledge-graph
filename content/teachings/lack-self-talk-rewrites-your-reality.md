Joe argues that the inner narration of lack — "not enough money, not enough money" repeated hundreds of times a day — operates exactly like having someone call you an idiot all day long. It reshapes your self-definition, and that self-definition then determines what you can perceive.

> "If you tell yourself every day, hundreds of times a day, 'You're an idiot. You're an idiot.' Obviously it's going to affect the way that you see yourself."

The cost isn't only how you feel; it's what you can see. A mind running "possibilities, possibilities" notices the opportunities around it, while a mind running "not enough" filters those same opportunities out. Reality gets bent to match the story. Joe illustrates this with a friend who constantly called her child "naughty" — the child was literally learning to be naughty, and his behavior improved over six months once she simply stopped the label.

> "How we define ourselves is often how we live."

The lack-voice isn't a neutral report on your circumstances. It's an active force defining you, and through that definition, shaping the reality you live in.
