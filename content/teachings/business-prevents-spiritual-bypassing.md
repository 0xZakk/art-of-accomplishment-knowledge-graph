Joe identifies several unique advantages of business as a spiritual practice over traditional practices like meditation or somatic work. The core advantage: you can't fool yourself easily. In meditation, you can achieve a blissful state and then snap at a child who interrupts. In business, the rubber hits the road every day.

Three specific ways business prevents bypassing: First, you can't maintain a fantasy of separation — "if I could just meditate in a cave, I'd be happy" — because the business would fail. Second, there's less room for spiritual navel-gazing because constant self-focus leads to business failure. Third, results are visible and immediate — if your defensiveness is blocking good ideas, you'll see the consequences in the business.

> "You can fool yourself into a somatic experience. You can fool yourself into a state of meditation and then a little kid comes up and bothers you and you're like get out of my way kid."

> "In business if you constantly think about yourself you're gonna fail. So there's something really beautiful about that — the business keeps it really real."

This makes business a uniquely honest mirror for self-development.
