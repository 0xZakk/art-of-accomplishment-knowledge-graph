The moment Joe says most people miss is the one happening right under their nose: how much has already changed. Theo hedges with "I think I want a new story," and Joe points out that the hedge itself proves the change. You don't reach this level of questioning unless the story has already shifted. The very fact that Theo can call it "a story" means he's separated from it.

> "You don't get to this level of questioning unless the story is actually probably already changed."

The trouble is that in the early stages, transformation is invisible from the inside. The new behavior — pausing, being vulnerable, being true to himself in front of people instead of performing for them — doesn't feel like a milestone. It just feels like the moment. So people keep waiting for change while standing inside it, which slows them down because they don't trust what's already underway.

> "This is the amazing thing about transformation. When you're transforming, especially in the early stages, you can't even recognize... that's just the way it works."
