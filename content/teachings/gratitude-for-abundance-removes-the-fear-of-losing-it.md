Joe's gratitude practice came full circle. It began as a way out of poverty consciousness — proving to himself there was no lack. Now, with plenty of money, the practice has a different function. He's not using gratitude to demonstrate he isn't lacking; he's grateful for what is, and that gratitude shows him there's nothing to fear in losing it.

> "It wasn't like I'm showing gratitude to show that I don't have lack. I'm having gratitude for the financial situation we have. It shows me that oh, there's nothing to be scared of in losing it. I've been poor before. That's not a problem."

Having been poor before, he knows losing the money wouldn't break him. That knowledge lets him receive abundance without being captured by it.

> "It allows me to receive it in such a way that I don't get defined by it. And that was the thing that I was always scared of when I was a kid."

The childhood fear was becoming his father — defined by money, forced to protect it, made into someone by it. Gratitude is what lets him hold money without letting it hold him.
