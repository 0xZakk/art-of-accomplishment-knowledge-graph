When Joe found the handful of people who could create real change in a single conversation, they worked in wildly different ways — some mean, some gentle, some "like watching somebody with a scalpel," some "like watching them with a hatchet." Yet underneath the stylistic differences, every one of them shared the same four characteristics, which AOA calls view: vulnerability, impartiality, empathy, and wonder.

> "Some was like watching somebody with a scalpel and some it was like watching them with a hatchet. But the thing that was true about all of them is that they all had these four characteristics."

Vulnerability, in this definition, isn't weakness — it's saying the slightly scary thing and taking the risk yourself rather than demanding it of the other person. Impartiality means not trying to get anyone anywhere, trusting the wisdom across from you. Empathy means being with the person and feeling them without buying their story or collapsing into "woe is you" toxic empathy. Wonder means staying in awe rather than in a knowing state — even curiosity is suspect, because curiosity hunts for an answer.

The pattern held across every effective practitioner Joe found, which is what convinced him these four qualities were the real engine of transformation, not the particular words or style any of them used.
