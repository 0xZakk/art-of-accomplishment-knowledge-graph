The deepest reframe in this conversation is on commitment itself. You can't commit to staying together forever, because that requires abandoning yourself if things turn bad. A healthy relationship asks you to neither enmesh nor leave — and the commitment that walks that line is a commitment to stay in connection.

> "It's I'm literally going to stay in connection with you... Even when I'm leaving, I'm staying in connection with you."

This is not a commitment to a fairy-tale relationship, to never fighting, to avoiding conflict, or to a job that looks good on LinkedIn. It's the commitment to stay in dialogue: "I'm not going to abandon you, but I'm not going to abandon myself in every moment." You can even leave a relationship or a company while keeping that connection intact — telling someone clearly that something was toxic without falling into them or cutting them off.

> "I'm not leaving you, and I'm not leaving myself."

And because not-enmeshing and not-abandoning while staying in relationship constantly forces you to refine what works and what doesn't, this kind of commitment produces growth whether you like it or not.
