Tara Healey defines somatic awareness simply: knowing how your body feels at any given moment and developing an ongoing relationship with it. That basic skill, she argues, is a doorway to several things at once — presence, wholeness with yourself, better listening to others, and access to the wisdom of your emotions.

> "Somatic awareness which really just means how does your body feel and developing a relationship with the body and becoming aware of how your body feels at any point is a key to presence."

Most of us were trained to locate ourselves in the head, where it felt safe to live. Tara names herself as a former straight-A "brainiak" who had effectively closed her body off and lost touch with her emotions. The cost of that arrangement is information: when attention stays in the head, you lose the data of your emotional process and the body's quieter knowing.

> "The body is a beautiful hacked presence... but we lose tons of information in doing so about our emotional process and about the wisdom of the body."

The remedy isn't to abandon the mind but to build a relationship with the body — to be able to drop attention down into the gut, the chest, or the hands and actually feel what's there. As that relationship deepens, emotions that were cut off become available again, and a more intuitive way of moving through the world becomes possible.
