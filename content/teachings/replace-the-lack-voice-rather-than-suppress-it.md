Telling yourself to stop a habit almost never works. Joe points out that "stop eating so much" or "stop playing so many video games" rarely changes anything — and the same is true for the constant inner monologue of lack. You can't simply will it into silence.

> "Unfortunately, when we tell ourselves to stop doing something, it rarely freaking works."

Because suppression fails, the voice has to be replaced with something else, and that something is gratitude. Gratitude is the active competitor to the "you need more, you don't have enough" narration that runs perpetually in the background. You are, as Joe puts it, literally competing with the not-enough voice — and you win not by fighting it but by installing a louder, truer voice in its place. This is why the practice has to be done repeatedly over days, weeks, and months: it's overwriting a habit of self-talk that itself runs hundreds of times a day.

> "You are literally needing to compete with the not enough voice in your head."
