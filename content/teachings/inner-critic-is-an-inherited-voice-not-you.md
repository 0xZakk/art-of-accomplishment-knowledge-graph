The negative voice in your head feels like you talking to you, but Joe argues it isn't. It's an old tape of someone else — usually whoever's criticism you absorbed as a small child. He illustrates this with his daughter, who on a road trip recognized that her inner voice spoke in the exact tone and words of her grandmother and her teacher, the two people besides her parents she spent the most time with.

> "It's not you talking to you, it's somebody else talking to you. It's like an old tape of somebody talking to you and you're talking to yourself like you're three or four years old."

He offers a concrete way to trace yours: sit down for thirty minutes, write everything the voice says, then ask "who said that to me as a kid?" The answer reveals its origin. The unsettling part is the asymmetry — you're a full-grown adult listening to a voice that's addressing the three- or four-year-old version of you, and treating its advice as gospel.
