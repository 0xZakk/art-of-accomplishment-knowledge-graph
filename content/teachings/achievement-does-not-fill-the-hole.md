Joe has watched the same thing happen across hundreds of high performers: they reach the dream and discover it didn't fill the gap. The world's number one golfer says it plainly — winning is fulfilling from the sense of accomplishment, but it does not touch the deepest places of the heart. Joe has met hundred-millionaires who are miserable, asking the same question.

> "I cannot tell you how many people I've met who have gotten their dreams and then they're like, wait, I got everything I wanted, but it's not actually what I needed."

The mechanism is that the head always asks "now what?" the instant you arrive. Win two majors and the very next question is about the next tournament. Grow a business and people ask about the next one. The achievement was supposed to be the destination, but the structure that drove you toward it simply relocates the finish line. The hole stays exactly where it was.

> "You think that you're going to be fulfilled by money or by people loving you or by fame... and then you get all that stuff and you're like, whoa, the hole is not filled."
