A common fantasy about awakening is that your humanity dies along with your separate self—that you'll float above your old wounds, always feel good, and be done with the messy emotional material. Joe punctures this directly. You can have genuine non-dual access and still be carrying every bit of your unprocessed history.

> "Before awakening daddy issues, after awakening daddy issues."

In fact, awakening tends to make you *more* human, not less. Without the option of dissociating into spacious awareness, you're left with a more sensitive system that simply can't tolerate what it used to. Some bad habits fall away instantly; the ones that remain become far more uncomfortable. This is the hidden danger of the head awakening especially: it lets people supersede their emotions so cleanly that they skip the trauma work that would actually free them—what a teacher of Joe's called "dragging them back into hell," though it turns out to be heaven.

The upside of that sensitivity is clarity. When something doesn't feel aligned, the response becomes immediate rather than a pros-and-cons list checked against sixteen people: "Oh no, this doesn't work for me and I'm going to step into" the action that fits, without worrying whether it matches who you've been.
