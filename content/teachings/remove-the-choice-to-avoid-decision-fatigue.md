The marshmallow experiment reveals something unexpected: the children who succeeded didn't resist temptation through willpower—they looked away from the marshmallow entirely. They removed the decision from their field of awareness. Joe applies this to adult habits: if your Xbox controller is always visible, you're making the decision to play or not play hundreds of times, and eventually willpower fails.

The solution is environmental design: give the controller to someone else, hide it, restructure your environment so the tempting choice simply isn't available. This is discipline through architecture rather than effort. You don't need willpower if you don't face the choice.

This connects to Joe's broader insight that structural changes are more effective than direct interventions—change the system, not the person.
