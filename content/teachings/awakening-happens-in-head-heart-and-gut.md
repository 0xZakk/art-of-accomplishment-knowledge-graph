Joe parses awakening into three flavors—head, heart, and gut—while insisting they are all the same movement, just blooming at different times for different people, "just like you'd have a flower bed." In every case the sense of self dissolves; the three names simply track which doorway it dissolves through.

The head awakening comes from seeing through the thought of "I." Joe asks: if your body, cells, emotions, and thoughts have all changed since you were seven, how is there still a "you"? The same seeing-through happens to people who live in ideas—physicists and philosophers who watch every thought turn out to be true and false at once until they can no longer believe their own thinking. The heart awakening works through emotions rather than thoughts: when you welcome every emotion as both true and false, the self dissolves into boundless love.

> "When you love, it disintegrates self just as much as identification of awareness in the mind."

The gut, or nervous-system, awakening is the hardest to describe and the most dangerous to force—Joe associates it with kundalini and the recognition that "you're inherently safe," where "the me against the world on the nervous system level starts to disintegrate." Each path arrives at the same place: the seeing-through of a separate self.
