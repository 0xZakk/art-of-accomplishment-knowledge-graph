The fear of being seen is self-fulfilling. Because you show up scared rather than confident, people respond to the fear—and you read their ordinary curiosity as rejection, collecting evidence that being seen is dangerous. Joe calls this accumulating loop the golden algorithm.

> "The emotion that we are scared to have we are avoiding the exact way that we are actually inviting it in. So I'm scared to be seen and therefore like when people see me they see this scared thing and I don't get the love that I want. See I've proven that I'm no good."

There's a second, sharper irony. When you fear what someone will think of you, you've already done to them everything you're afraid they'll do to you—judged them, boxed them, made them an enemy who's out to hurt you.

> "If I'm scared about what you're going to say about me I have judged you, I have categorized you, I've boxed you, I've made you into an enemy... which is everything you're scared that they're going to do to you, you've already done to them."

The hiding doesn't protect you from rejection. It manufactures it—first by making you unreceivable, and second by having you reject the other person before they ever speak.
