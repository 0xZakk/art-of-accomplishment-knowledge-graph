When you work on all three levels — head, heart, and nervous system — transformation can happen quite fast. Joe points out that fast change is itself destabilizing. Decades-old patterns coming undone, big emotional releases, and large nervous system movements are not things to do alone or casually.

> "Because often times when you're transforming on all three levels, that transformation can happen quite quickly. And again, that can feel a little destabilizing."

That's why he insists the work happen inside a good container: a group of highly trustworthy people who really understand what they're doing and can hold you while you move. The container isn't a nicety; it's what makes it safe to go into the deeper somatic and emotional layers at all. The progression he describes — talking material first, then experiential courses, then in-person deep dives — exists precisely so people aren't dropped into the most destabilizing work before they're ready for it.
