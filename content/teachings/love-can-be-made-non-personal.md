The breakthrough in this session isn't about ending the love but about un-trapping it. The widow has the love bound up in one person — it's "for him," for "us." Joe invites her to keep all of that and also make the love non-personal: to let it be for everything and everyone, not just the one man it was attached to.

> "Make the love that you have for him non-personal. Have it be for everything and everyone."

He pulls her out of the story and into her body to actually do it, not just understand it. The instruction is concrete: see the world the way he saw her, attune to the world the way he attuned to her. The specific way she was loved becomes a template for how she can love everything.

> "So I don't have to trap this love for him. I can expand it to the world is what you're saying."

The final move is holding both at once — the personal love for him and the non-personal love for the world — and discovering they aren't in competition. She lands in both simultaneously and finds it beautiful. Love doesn't have to be rationed between the past and the world; it can be both at the same time.
