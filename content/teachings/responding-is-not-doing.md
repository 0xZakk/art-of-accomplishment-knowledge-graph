Every email answered, every Slack responded to, every meeting attended gives you a micro-hit of completion. Done. Done. Done. Your brain registers progress. But at the end of the day—did anything actually change?

> "I'm answering emails but I'm not doing anything... my mind was being tricked by a response that feels good but I was a video game."

The platforms are designed for this. Each one is incentivized to engage you, not to make your life better. They reward responsiveness, not impact. And the cultural layer reinforces it: in large corporations, people respond to meetings all day, feel productive, and the company hasn't grown in two years.

The distinction is between pressing enter (responding) and pressing forward (creating change). If nothing changed after an hour of emails, that hour could have been spent on something that would have changed everything.
