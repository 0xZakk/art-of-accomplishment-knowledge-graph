At the end of the session, Joe gives Harry a simple, efficient practice. Rather than trying to engineer more moments of presence or figure out the rule for when he stops judging himself, Harry should simply be grateful each time he notices one of those moments has already happened.

> "The trick is to be grateful every time you recognize one of those moments... just being grateful for those moments will make the whole thing be more efficient."

The mechanism is that gratitude reinforces and embeds the experience in the body rather than leaving it as an intellectual note. When Harry recalls the moment two minutes earlier where he was present without the constant editor, Joe has him feel grateful for it on the spot. The gratitude isn't a reward for good behavior; it's what lets the felt experience of self-acceptance settle in and repeat, speeding up the whole transformation without effort or force.

> "Feel grateful that there was this moment where you let yourself be you without the chain of the consistent editor."
