Joe identifies a contradiction running underneath a lot of stuck striving. Evolution wired us to fit in—to be normal, to belong to the group. But we also want to be extraordinary. The two wants are mutually exclusive, and most people never notice the conflict.

> "Anybody who's striving for that extraordinariness never puts it together that that would make them stand out, it would make them exactly not ordinary and not fit in."

Because the two pulls run at the same time, they produce friction rather than motion. You reach for the extraordinary and then, often unconsciously, pull yourself back toward safety and sameness. The result is a lot of effort that never quite lands.

> "They want to go somewhere but they don't ever get there because they have these two competing wants."

The insight isn't to pick one over the other but to see the hidden double-bind clearly. As long as the desire to belong and the desire to stand out are fighting inside the same system, action stays misaligned and the person spins in place—wanting to go somewhere they've quietly made it impossible to arrive.
