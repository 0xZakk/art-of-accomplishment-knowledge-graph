The antidote to self-rejection isn't to argue yourself into self-worth. It's to look honestly for what's actually worth rejecting—and find nothing. Joe asks the man to search inside himself for anything genuinely deserving of rejection, and the search comes up empty.

> "I want you to look inside yourself and see if you can find anything worth rejecting. Anything."

When nothing turns up, the only move left is to welcome the whole thing—the sadness, the resistance, all of it—rather than push any part away.

> "So then welcome it. Welcome the whole thing just for like one minute. See what it's like to welcome yourself completely."

Welcoming is not a technique for fixing yourself; it's the recognition that there was never anything to fix. The instant you stop hunting for the flaw that justifies rejection, the rejection has nothing to stand on. What was being held down—in this case, ambition—is then free to rise.
