A specific and persistent kind of anxiety comes from not being who you are. If you're saying things that aren't true, manipulating instead of being direct with your wants, or suppressing the emotions you know you need to feel, the system registers a low-grade, ongoing threat. This isn't about failing to do tasks—it's about failing to be a person.

> "This isn't a you can't do what you want to do. This is a can't be who you are. Being is an emotional experience. We feel being."

When you can't let yourself feel the full range—aliveness, joy, pain, sorrow, wonder, exuberance—a natural anxiety sets in. Joe traces the logic underneath it: the only reason to suppress who you are is the belief that being yourself will get you in trouble.

> "The only reason not to be what I want to be is because somehow I'm going to get in trouble. And if I could get in trouble at any minute, it means I'm constantly under threat. And of course, I'm anxious then."

The anxiety isn't irrational. It's the accurate readout of a life lived under the constant threat of being punished for existing as yourself.
