The primary fear about AI — losing your job — exposes the deeper problem: we value ourselves through the lens of productivity. Joe argues the fear is an accurate reading of a society that has no value system to honor a person beyond their output, so losing work feels like losing the ability to care for your family even where AI might create abundance.

> "One of the best outcomes I could see happening from all of this is to deeply uproot the societal belief system that we are worth what we can do rather than being worth who we are."

But Joe is skeptical that this shift can happen by argument. You can't talk someone out of equating worth with production if they have nothing else to feel valuable for. He gives humanity "almost no capacity" to stop thinking of itself as a value of production until people actually feel a different way of being — the felt experience of being valuable simply because they are. The intellectual reframe is empty until it's embodied.

> "The only way I think that's actually going to happen is if people can feel their worth in another way."

This is why the felt sense of family, team, and community matters: it's the only thing that can replace productivity as a source of worth, because it offers something to feel valuable for that has nothing to do with what you can do.
