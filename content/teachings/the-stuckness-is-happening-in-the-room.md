The man wants to talk about how to move his life forward. Joe redirects him to something far more immediate: the same dynamic that has him stuck in life is playing out, live, in the conversation they're having.

> "This thing that you're dealing with in this moment is the thing to be addressed. It's not how do I move my life forward. It's like what's creating this?"

The wanting-help-but-resisting-help, the confusion, the "I'm trying"—it's all happening in real time, with Joe, exactly as it happens with the marketers and with life. That's why the present moment is where the work is. You don't need to reconstruct the man's whole history; the pattern is right here, available to be felt and seen.

> "It's happening again right now. You're like I want your help but I don't want your help. It comes as confusion."

By treating the room as the laboratory, Joe collapses the gap between the abstract problem and its living mechanism. The stuckness isn't a story about the past or the future—it's a thing the man is doing now, which means it can be undone now.
