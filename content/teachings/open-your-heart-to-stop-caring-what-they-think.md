When you're scared of being seen, you've stopped love and connection from flowing—you've objectified the other person, turned them into an object in your game of feeling safe. The counterintuitive move is to actively open your heart back toward them, and Joe insists this is the one technique that works almost instantly.

> "If I choose to be connected with you, it doesn't really matter if you're connected to me for my experience."

The reason it works is that your concern with someone's judgment depends on a closed, disconnected stance toward them. Restore the connection from your side and the concern simply has nowhere to live. As Joe puts it, you can connect with someone who isn't even connecting back, and notice how much less you care what they think.

Opening the heart also flips concern into wonder. Instead of bracing for what they think of you, you get curious about what they're actually experiencing—which opens the nervous system, restores a sense of safety, and lets in new information. Used as a wonder practice ("how upset are you at me right now?"), it reliably reveals that most of what you feared about the other person was never true. This is Joe's single concrete instruction for the freeze moment: open your heart to that person.
