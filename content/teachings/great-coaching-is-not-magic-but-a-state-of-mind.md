The first time Joe watched someone coach — a Dutch consultant named Case Debron who could sit with one person after another and consistently change each of their lives — his mind was blown. He hadn't thought such a thing was even possible; his idea of coaching was small changes over weeks and years. Watching Case transform person after person in a single evening, he assumed it was magic.

> "I thought it was magic, just like a lot of people who watch me coach think that it's magic. But it wasn't magic."

Rather than leave it as mystery, Joe got determined to figure out what was actually happening. After Case died, he traveled the world and found four or five people who could create significant change in a very short time. The discovery was that what looked like magic was reproducible — it rested on a shared set of qualities and a state of mind, not on some innate gift or trick.

This reframing matters because it makes life-changing conversation learnable. If it's magic, you either have it or you don't. If it's a state of mind built from years of inner work and a specific way of holding people, anyone willing to embody it can develop it.
