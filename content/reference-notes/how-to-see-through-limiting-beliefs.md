## Summary

Joe Hudson and Brett Kistler dive deeper into how to see through limiting beliefs, building on a prior episode that defined them. Joe distinguishes two types: beliefs you haven't yet recognized as limiting (which can dissolve instantly once seen), and beliefs you intellectually recognize but that still grip your body and behavior. The key insight is that limiting beliefs aren't about truth or falsity — they're identified by the constriction they create in your system.

Joe outlines three foundational categories of beliefs that keep other limiting beliefs stuck: "I'm bad" (shame-based self-judgment), "it's hard" (the assumption that change requires suffering), and "I'll be unsafe" (fear of abandonment, loss, or consequences). Each of these meta-beliefs reinforces the others and prevents integration. He also introduces the concept of a "lattice work" of beliefs — interconnected layers that must be seen through together for real change.

The episode emphasizes that seeing through beliefs is about creating spaciousness rather than proving beliefs wrong. Joe notes that everyone has already changed thousands of limiting beliefs naturally over their lifetime without effort or awareness, suggesting the process is far easier than we assume. The ultimate guidance is to trust awareness itself — if you truly see through a belief, you don't need to do anything extra to change it, just as you don't need to remind yourself that two plus two equals four.
