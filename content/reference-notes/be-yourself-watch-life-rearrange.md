## Summary

Joe shares a story that shook him: a dying woman's last words to her daughter were "All my life I felt like there was something wrong with me. What a waste." This becomes the foundation for understanding regret.

Regret comes not from outcomes, but from contorting ourselves to get outcomes. When we're 100% ourselves—when we feel proud of how we behaved regardless of results—there's no regret. And being ourselves designs a life where people who say yes are saying yes to the real us.

- [[literature-notes/regret-comes-from-not-being-yourself|Regret comes from not being yourself]]

## Key Quotes

> "All my life I felt like there was something wrong with me. What a waste."

> "If we are ourselves, then the things that say yes to us are the things that are saying yes to us, not the contorted version of ourselves."

> "If you are regretting a conversation, you are very likely going to regret the day. If you regret the day, you're very likely going to regret the year. If you regret the year, you're very likely going to regret your life."

> "If you just be yourself, you'll realize there's nothing wrong with you. You'll realize that there is a whole world designed for you waiting for you to be you."
