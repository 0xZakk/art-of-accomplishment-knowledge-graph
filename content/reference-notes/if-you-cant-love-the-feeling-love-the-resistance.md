## Summary

Joe Hudson and Brett Kistler explore the nature of resistance in personal transformation. Drawing on a David White essay about denial, they examine how resistance is not an obstacle to overcome but a natural and necessary part of the transformational process — like tension in a living body or a tree growing toward light. Joe emphasizes that fighting resistance only creates more resistance, and the real invitation is to love the resistance rather than trying to eliminate it.

The conversation moves through practical territory including procrastination, where Joe explains how negative self-reinforcement creates an AC-current loop — every time you think about the task, you punish yourself, training your nervous system to avoid it. The antidote is not willpower but curiosity: what is the feeling underneath the resistance? What is procrastination without resistance in it? Brett discovers that his procrastination dissolves into an impulse to act when the resistance is removed.

Joe shares his personal story of a pot habit in his twenties that dissolved without willpower once he stopped fighting it and instead felt the underlying sadness he'd been avoiding. He concludes that resistance can never be fully eliminated — it is life itself — and the real question is how to hold it with the most gentleness and love possible.
