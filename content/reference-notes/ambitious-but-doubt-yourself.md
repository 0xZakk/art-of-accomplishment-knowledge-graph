## Summary

Joe works with highly successful people in Silicon Valley—they all have doubt too. The difference is they know what to do with the signal instead of letting it stop them.

Doubt arises in two situations: when thinking about taking action, and when getting unwanted reactions. In both cases, doubt protects you from emotions you don't want to feel—either past pain (criticism as a child) or future fear (failure or success).

The doubt isn't about logic. It's about emotions.

## Key Concepts

- [[literature-notes/doubt-protects-from-unwanted-emotions|Doubt protects you from emotions you don't want to feel]]
- [[literature-notes/fear-of-success-creates-doubt-too|Fear of success creates doubt just like fear of failure]]

## Key Quotes

> "Very successful people have doubt too. The only difference is they listen to that signal and know what to do with it rather than let it stop them."

> "You have an ambition, which means you want to move something forward, and you're scared there's going to be a feeling that arises that you don't want to feel. The doubt is coming up as a way to protect you from that feeling."

> "The doubt is doing is slowing your ambition so you don't have to feel the thing."
