## Summary

This is the first of a two-part series on money. Joe and Brett explore the common misconception that money is our savior — that if we just had more of it, it would solve all our problems. Joe shares a pivotal moment in a hammock in El Salvador where he realized he'd made money a surrogate for his father's love, and that his relationship with money tracked his relationship with his father through different phases.

The episode explores how money becomes a surrogate for love, security, power, community, and experience. Joe describes how a gratitude practice shifted his relationship with money, leading to the insight that billionaires and non-billionaires share the same mental pattern of "not enough." They discuss the difference between resources and resourcefulness, how the monetary system is structurally built to feel deficient, and how the fear of losing motivation keeps people trapped on the treadmill of acquisition. The conversation touches on money as "the kinkiest thing on the planet" — more taboo than sexuality — and how shame around money (whether having too much or too little) creates disconnection. Joe emphasizes that one's relationship with money mirrors one's relationship with self, and that acceptance of the system (like acceptance of a person) is the most effective path to changing it.
