## Summary

Joe identifies three things that keep people poor: (1) beliefs about money—thinking it's bad, or being needy about it; (2) doing work for money that isn't aligned with who you are, making money a source of oppression rather than empowerment; and (3) defining yourself as someone who doesn't have enough.

He shares his personal story of going from $40,000 in debt to $60,000 in the bank in under 8 months by shifting his identity from "someone who doesn't have enough" to "someone who feels grateful for what they have." The key insight is that wealthy people didn't define themselves by what they lacked—they defined themselves as the person who could create wealth.

## Key Quotes

> "I went from $40,000 in debt to $60,000 in the bank in under 8 months simply because I had changed my idea of myself from somebody who doesn't have enough to somebody who feels so grateful and lucky for everything that they do have."

> "If you think money is bad, you're probably not going to want to have it in your life."

> "Money is like paint—it's an art form that I get to use to make an expression of something that's important to me in the world."

> "They didn't think 'I don't have enough.' They thought 'I'm the person who's going to make a billion dollars.' That's how they defined themselves."

> "It's not just about getting money. There's a lot of people who are miserable with a lot of money. It's about knowing yourself and building your wealth that way."
