## Summary

Joe Hudson and Brett Kessler dismantle the common question "How do I find my purpose?" by showing that the question itself assumes you don't already have one. Joe observes that people who are truly thriving never talk about having "found" their purpose — they're just living it. The search for purpose often masks a search for approval: "What can I be acknowledged for?"

The core reframe is that purpose isn't an object to find but an emergent property of how you're being. Joe traces a thread through his own life — music, comic strips, business, coaching — and realizes the common element was never *what* he did but *how* he did it: always exploring how humans see themselves. Purpose lives in the present moment, in the quality of engagement, not in a career title or mission statement.

The conversation traces a maturation arc: first there's fear and aliveness in stepping toward purpose, then scary choicelessness where it hurts too much to do anything else, and finally a non-personal surrender where purpose lives through you. Joe emphasizes that this journey can't be shortcut — trying to jump ahead is like kids wanting to be older.
