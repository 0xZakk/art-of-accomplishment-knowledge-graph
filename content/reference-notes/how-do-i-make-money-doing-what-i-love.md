## Summary

In this brief coaching clip, Joe works with a public speaking coach who loves his work but can't seem to make money doing it. Through inquiry, Joe traces the man's money issues back to a childhood experience: at age seven, his alcoholic father made a deal to pay him $200 each weekend he stayed sober. When the boy proudly bragged about the $1,200 he'd accumulated, his father raged—and the boy locked in the belief that "money is more important than I am."

Joe reframes this: it wasn't money that was more important—it was the father's avoidance of his own helplessness and shame. Joe then shows the man how he's recreating this pattern by making shame and avoidance more important than himself, avoiding the marketing he already knows how to do because of shame around self-promotion.

## Key Quotes

> "When I love what I'm doing I don't make any money. And when I hate what I'm doing I've made lots and lots of money."

> "How much are you making shame more important than you?"

> "Either you're really a crappy coach, or you are not marketing yourself. I'm going to assume it's the second."

> "If you know how to do public speaking, you know how to market yourself. It's the same principles."
