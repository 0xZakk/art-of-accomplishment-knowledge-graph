## Summary

Joe presents three techniques for leading without managing, plus the underlying mindset. First, say what you want cleanly—leaders often don't state their wants out of guilt, fear of selfishness, or rebellion against authority, but the leader's wants are the most aligned with the organization's needs, and people feel safe when they know what's wanted. Second, speak to the problem without solving it—name what doesn't feel right and let the team create the solution, because solutions people create are solutions they're aligned to execute. Third, only work with great people—anyone not performing doesn't enjoy it either, so it's about finding the right match.

The underlying mindset: keep the perspective on the team, not on yourself. You say what you want because that's what the team needs. You hire great people because that's how the team thrives. You speak to problems without solving them because that's how you empower the team.
