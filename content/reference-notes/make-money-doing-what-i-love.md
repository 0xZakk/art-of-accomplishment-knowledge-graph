## Summary

A man shares that he makes money when he hates what he's doing but can't make money when he loves his work — currently as a public speaking coach. Joe traces this back to a childhood memory: at seven years old, the man's alcoholic father had a deal to pay him $200 every weekend he stayed sober. When the boy bragged about the $1,200 he'd accumulated, his father flew into a rage, ending the deal.

Joe helps the man see that it wasn't actually money that was more important than him — it was his father's avoidance of helplessness and shame. The man locked in a belief that money is more important than he is. Joe points out that as a public speaking coach, he already knows how to market himself — it's the same principles he teaches — but shame is preventing him from doing so.
