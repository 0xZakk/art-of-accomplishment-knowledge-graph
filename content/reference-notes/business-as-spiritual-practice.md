## Summary

Joe and Brett discuss how business can serve as a spiritual practice — a path of self-discovery rather than just a means to an end. Joe argues that aligning business with who you truly are is "like sweeping thousand dollar bills off the floor." The formula involves three prongs: embracing intensity (going into difficulty rather than avoiding it), deepening connection (with self, others, customers, employees), and discerning internal alignment (noticing what feels off and correcting it).

They explore why business is uniquely suited as a developmental tool: you can't fool yourself easily because the rubber hits the road every day, there's no false separation possible (you can't retreat to a cave), and there's less room for spiritual navel-gazing because results are visible. Joe gives examples of how second-guessing, conflict avoidance, and defensiveness in business all trace to the same emotional patterns that show up in personal development work.

Joe also discusses the art of business — that business is a creative expression like a symphony, constantly changing and affecting everyone who interacts with it. The episode touches on how gratitude in business meetings shifts problem-solving from "what's wrong" to "what's right," and how moving from power to empowerment transforms leadership.
