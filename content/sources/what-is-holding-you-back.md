## Summary

In this short promotional video for his Master Class, Joe Hudson identifies the core reason people fail to accomplish long-standing goals: they beat themselves up for not doing it. Whether the goal is starting a business, exercising more, or deepening relationships, the pattern is the same—shame-based motivation that keeps people stagnant rather than moving forward.

Joe introduces the idea that there are seven ways people hold themselves back, with shame being the primary one. He contrasts shame-based pushing with genuine motivation, arguing that redirecting the energy spent on self-criticism toward self-motivation would dramatically increase effectiveness. The Master Class promises to help participants recognize, see through, and move beyond these seven limitations using simple tools.

## Key Concepts

- [[teachings/shame-stagnates-behavior|Shame keeps you stagnant rather than motivating change]]

## Key Quotes

> "There's something that you've been trying to do for years that you have not done and I can tell you why you haven't done it without even knowing what it is."

> "Shame keeps you stagnant. It holds you back and it keeps you cycling over the same thing."

> "Imagine if you spend as much time motivating yourself as you did beating yourself up, how much more productive would you be."

> "Motivating yourself without shame works far better."
