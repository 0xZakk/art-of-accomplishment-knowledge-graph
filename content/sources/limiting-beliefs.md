## Summary

Brett and Joe do a deep dive into limiting beliefs — ideas that limit your capacity to be yourself, get what you want, or be happy. Joe identifies three categories: false thoughts (believing something untrue, like Joe's father who believed he failed as a CEO when the company was actually shut down by its parent for being too successful), misinterpretation of experience (the amusement ride story where terror and exhilaration are the same physical experience interpreted differently), and thinking you know the whole story (the Covey subway story where judgment instantly transforms into compassion when new information arrives).

The episode explores how to find limiting beliefs (physical constriction, binary thinking, feeling trapped, stagnation) and how to see through them. Joe emphasizes this is a realization methodology, not behavior control — you don't get to choose which specific outcomes change, but you reliably gain more freedom and joy. The tools include inquiry, emotional fluidity, and allowing pleasure/safety into the nervous system.
