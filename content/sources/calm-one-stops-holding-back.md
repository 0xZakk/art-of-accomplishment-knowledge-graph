## Summary

In this coaching session, Joe works with a man who wants to express himself but is terrified that being direct will come across as aggressive. The man can feel a "power inside" that he's constantly holding back, and he equates any internal frustration or agitation with failing to be calm. Joe pokes at the logic: the man believes he can only act when there's no internal conflict, yet he's never had a single moment in the last year without internal conflict—and he's acted anyway.

Joe reframes the whole setup as a bad trade. By keeping up the appearance of calm, the man is trading away his joy, his power, and his love. Worse, Joe points out, he probably isn't even reading as calm—he's reading as distant. The man comes to see that "me" isn't the problem to be managed; "me" is the energy of love and joy dying to come out.

In the final stretch, Joe traces the pattern to its childhood root: the man's sister was the free, expressive, rebellious one who got in trouble, and his job became to stay out of the line of fire and help everyone settle afterward. The whole strategy of muting his love, power, and joy was a way to stay safe. The session ends with Joe inviting him to give his own racing heart the love he refuses to let anyone hold back.
