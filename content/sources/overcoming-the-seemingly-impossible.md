## Summary

Heather Falenski, founder and CEO of One World Media, spent nearly a decade producing documentaries in African conflict zones and adventure sports. The cumulative stress led to chronic illness and complex PTSD. Years of trying to control her way out—therapists, meditation, rest, health protocols—all failed. She hit rock bottom when every tool in her arsenal stopped working.

From that place of total surrender, she discovered what she calls "be your own light"—not as a platitude, but as a literal practice. Unable to change her outer circumstances, she began imagining what it would feel like to be healthy, connected, and fulfilled. At first it felt like dissociation or escape, but over time the "new suit" became more comfortable than the old one. She realized that holding a new self-concept steady internally eventually shifts the outer world to match.

The conversation explores how this practice works: stabilizing an inner state, enduring the "purge" of contradicting beliefs that surface, and the relationship between gratitude, identity, and reality. Heather emphasizes this is not mental gymnastics or forced positivity—it's catching the feeling of who you want to be before evidence arrives, and holding it steady through the emotional storms.
