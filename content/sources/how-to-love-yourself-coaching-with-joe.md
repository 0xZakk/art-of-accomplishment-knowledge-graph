## Summary

In this live coaching session, a young participant tells Joe she keeps getting overwhelmed by intense bodily sensations she can barely function around, says she hasn't felt good in years, and asks what to do about her emotions. Joe has her throw away the Notion document she's reading from and ask her question from her heart instead. Rather than trying to feel her whole body, he directs her to the single part that feels the worst, and to give that part exactly the love she would have wanted from her mom and dad — all the patience, not needing it to change.

The turning point comes when Joe asks whether she wants to feel good or to love herself, and she answers honestly that she wants to feel good. He affirms the honesty, and as she hangs out with the painful part and loves it, she becomes happy in the moment — directly contradicting her story that she's an unhappy person. Joe exposes the gap between the persistent narrative ("I'm an unhappy person") and the actual moment-to-moment experience, where happiness keeps showing up unnoticed because the overwhelm of unhappiness feels so loud.

He gives her two concepts for the road: when you start to love yourself, all the unloved parts come up to be loved (the cuttlefish example), and there's no rush — she'll be just as lovable in twenty years, so she can move at the most enjoyable pace. Her enjoyment becomes the reliable signal for when to go deeper into a feeling and when to take a break, since her nature is already to investigate.
