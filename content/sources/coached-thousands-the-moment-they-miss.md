## Summary

Joe breaks down a moment from Theo Von's podcast, where Theo wrestles aloud with the question from the Gospel of John — "Do you want to be healed?" Joe uses the clip to illuminate something he's watched thousands of people miss on the transformational journey: they can't recognize how much has already changed in them. Theo hedges — "I think I want a new story" — and Joe shows that the hedge itself, the willingness to sit in the question rather than speed past it, is the transformation already happening.

Along the way Joe names the self-abuse hidden in Theo's apologies ("I'm sorry to make this about me"), the childhood logic by which a mother who wouldn't meet his eyes left him concluding something was wrong with him, and the people-pleasing self that grew from delegating his worth to others. Crucially, Joe points out that the old wound is not the only story to release — the recent epiphany about that wound has itself become a rut. Every epiphany eventually becomes a future container if it isn't held lightly.

The throughline is trust. Joe argues that at a certain depth, transformation is as guaranteed as a toddler learning to walk; you don't have to force it, you only have to bring awareness to it and let that awareness settle. The "I have to let go" effort actually slows the process. The hungry craving for attention quiets not by getting more attention but by finally digesting the love and impact that are already there. And the fastest healing happens not in the polished story but at the raw edge — the heartbreak moment where Theo realizes he knows how to withhold love and doesn't want to anymore.
