When two people are in relationship—romantic, business, or otherwise—friction naturally arises as they grow. This friction isn't a sign that something is wrong. It's the natural tension of evolution.

> "That is the natural tension of evolution. And to avoid it you avoid the evolution. If you go into it then you speed the evolution."

Conflict avoidance doesn't prevent conflict—it stores it. The resentment builds like a slow burn until the eventual confrontation is far more explosive than the original tension would have been. Addressing friction weekly keeps it manageable. Avoiding it for months or years makes it relationship-ending.

The key distinction: this isn't about creating conflict or using conflict as a manipulation tool. It's about noticing the tension that's already there and leaning into it rather than away from it. The reward isn't winning—it's evolution that neither person could have imagined alone.
