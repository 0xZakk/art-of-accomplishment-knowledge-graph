Independently wealthy people who don't need to work are the least likely to recover from alcoholism. There's no "flaming field" — no acute pressure that forces change. They can just keep drinking. Joe applies this broadly: he rarely takes on extremely wealthy clients without a project because for many, inner work becomes entertainment rather than transformation.

The co-host shares his own experience: with more money than ever, he was free to do retreats and exploration, but certain things went unaddressed until everything crashed. The crash — laying people off, readjusting his lifestyle — initiated the deepest stage of personal growth. The comfort had enabled avoidance.

This doesn't mean suffering is required. But intensity — the kind that comes from having something real at stake — is often what pushes people along the path most effectively. When you can buy your way out of discomfort, you often buy your way out of growth too.
