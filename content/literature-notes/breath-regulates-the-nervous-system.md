When you're overwhelmed and can't process emotions in the moment — you're in a meeting, a hospital, a crisis — breath is the immediate tool. Slowing your breath, making it equal, calming the nervous system.

> "We can't control our nervous system but we can control our breath and our breath controls our nervous system."

Joe notes this is universal — yoga, Navy SEALs, same principle. But he warns that breath work can also be used to repress emotions. If someone keeps breathing to avoid ever feeling, "that can be a world of hurt." Breath is a bridge to buy time, not a replacement for emotional processing.
