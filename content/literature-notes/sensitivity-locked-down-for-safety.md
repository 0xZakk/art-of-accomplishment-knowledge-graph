Joe tells the client something she might not know about herself: her system is incredibly sensitive, with exceptional body awareness. But because it was so unsafe to have that much sensitivity, she learned to lock it all down. The composure mask isn't arbitrary — it's a survival strategy proportional to the intensity of what she feels.

> "Your system is incredibly sensitive... it was so sensitive that you learned to lock it down because it was so unsafe to have that sensitive of a system."

This reframes the problem entirely. The composure isn't a character flaw or weakness — it's evidence of enormous capacity. The very thing she's suppressing is an "incredible gift." But unlocking it requires gentleness and patience because the volume of sensation will be overwhelming at first. Joe advises her to "take it slowly because it's a lot."

The pattern is common: the people who lock down the hardest often have the most to feel. The defense is proportional to the gift. This means that the most armored, composed, controlled people may be the ones with the greatest sensitivity waiting underneath.
