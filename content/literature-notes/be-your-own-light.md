After years of chronic illness and complex PTSD, Heather Falenski discovered that nothing in her outer world was going to save her. No therapy, no rest, no health protocol. The realization that arrived was "be your own light"—not as a platitude but as a literal practice: generate from within the feeling of what you seek, before any external evidence arrives.

The practice involves entering a meditative state, imagining what it would feel like to be healthy, connected, and fulfilled—"trying on a new suit." At first the suit feels uncomfortable and unfamiliar. But held consistently, day after day, it becomes more natural than the old identity. This isn't positive thinking or emotional control—it's catching the essence or "vibe" of a different self-concept, while allowing all emotions to flow naturally.

> "I realized that nothing in my outer world was going to change my situation and that I couldn't wait on something to come from outside to save me."

The metaphor of wings captures it: birds don't worry about the branch breaking because they trust their wings. Being your own light means growing wings—becoming so internally secure that even betrayal or loss won't decimate you.
