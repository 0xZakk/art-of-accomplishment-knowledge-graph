When Joe was deeply aligned with what he wanted, there was no procrastination—just an addiction to the doing. He accomplished more doing one-tenth the work compared to grinding through tasks he "should" do.

> "The person who started the local tire shop and the person who started Google probably worked about the same amount of hours."

Effort isn't the differentiator. Alignment is. When your work is aligned with your genuine wants, energy is abundant and resistance disappears. When it's not, you need willpower, discipline, and productivity hacks—all of which are poor substitutes for actually wanting to do the thing.

Procrastination isn't a character flaw. It's feedback that something isn't aligned. Either the task doesn't matter, or you're approaching it from "should" rather than genuine desire.
