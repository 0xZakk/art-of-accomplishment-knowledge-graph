When Joe tells a blocked artist that art isn't important and it's never going to be enough, she says "that sucks." He responds: "That's freedom." She recognizes it immediately — it is freedom. But her habit is to make things hard, to require importance, to demand that it be enough.

Her teacher once told her "Doesn't matter. It's just art." It crushed her because she needed art to matter. That need for importance is itself the block. When you don't have to get anywhere, when it's not important, when it's never going to be enough — you're free to just create. No motivation required. No destination. No justification.

> "You don't have to get anywhere. It's not important and it's never going to be enough."

The moment she stops trying to make it important and drops into source, a piece that's been waiting 15 years comes alive. Freedom isn't in making art matter — it's in letting it not matter.
