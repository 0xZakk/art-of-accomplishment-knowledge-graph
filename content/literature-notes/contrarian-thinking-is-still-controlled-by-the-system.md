Rebellion against authority looks like independent thinking but is actually controlled by the very system it opposes. If you're rebelling, you're still defined by the thing you're rebelling against — you can't see truth in the original position, only the opposite. Joe discovered this in his own journey with questioning assumptions: his contrarian phase was really rebellion against his father, an authority figure.

> "I discovered the way that I was using it at that time was very much still in being controlled by the system because I was rebelling against something."

This pattern shows up everywhere. Self-described "contrarian investors" almost always have unresolved authority issues with their fathers. People who take on the identity of "I question everything" are often just adopting a different unquestioned worldview. True inquiry sees truth and lack of truth on both sides, rather than reflexively opposing one position.

The distinction matters: skepticism and debate aim to prove yourself right. Genuine questioning aims to see what's actually true — which requires being willing to find truth in the very thing you initially wanted to reject.
