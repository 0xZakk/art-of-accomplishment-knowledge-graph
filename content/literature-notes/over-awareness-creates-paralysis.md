A woman has been doing parts work — learning to identify her inner child, protector, and other sub-personalities — and it initially felt liberating. But now she's so aware of contradictory inner voices that she can't make decisions. Every gut reaction is immediately countered by another equally strong one. She's lost the natural flow she had before the awareness.

Joe compares this to making breathing conscious: "Imagine if breathing was something I had to think about all the time." Some processes work better under the hood. The parts framework is useful, but when it becomes the lens through which every decision must be filtered, it creates the very paralysis it was meant to resolve.

The resolution isn't less awareness but a different relationship to it. When she drops into her essential self — awareness without the parts framework — the spaciousness returns. The parts don't disappear; they just stop being the authority she's checking with. Joe asks: "What happened to all the parts?" She answers: "Not sure." And from that space, self-trust is no longer a question.
